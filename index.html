The Truth

When all four stages are pieced together, the truth behind the death at The Geminus Institute becomes clear - far darker and far more calculated than anything suggested in the facility's official reports.

The Experiment Beneath the Experiment

Jasmine and Jade were recruited into The Geminus Institute for a behavioural study comparing a twin with a diagnosed mental health disorder to one without.

But beneath the surface, the senior clinician, Dr Julia Smith, had been running a second, undisclosed line of research:

To artificially induce the symptoms of Jasmine's disorder in Jade - to prove it could be manufactured, controlled, replicated, and therefore "solved."

Smith justified it as a necessary push for scientific progress. The institution backed her, quietly protecting a study they viewed as groundbreaking, even if ethically catastrophic.

Jade's Discovery

Over several weeks, Jade began noticing changes in her perception, memory, and behaviour - abnormalities that didn't belong to her. Her secret investigation into medication logs, therapy notes, and missing documentation led her to the truth: she was being manipulated into illness.

And she didn't stay silent.

Jade confronted Dr Smith in private, threatening to expose the experiment unless she was restored to her normal mental state.

The Fatal Miscommunication

Panicked, Julia Smith sent a handwritten note instructing Jade to meet her in the courtyard after dark, claiming she would "correct the imbalance" and "fix the damage."

But the note never reached Jade.

Because of the twins' physical similarity, shared laundry, and their habit of borrowing clothing, Jasmine accidentally received the message - and wearing Jade's jumper, went instead, believing her sister was in danger.

In the dim courtyard, Dr Smith saw the jumper and assumed she was facing Jade - the twin who knew too much.

What followed was a brief, frantic confrontation. Jasmine tried to speak; Smith, in fear of exposure, lashed out.

A push, a stumble, a fall.

Jasmine's head struck a stone in the courtyard.

She died instantly.

The Institutional Cover-Up

The Geminus Institute moved fast.

They could not afford a scandal involving experimental malpractice, a dead patient, and an illegal research protocol.

  • Shift notes were altered.
  • Overdose materials were arranged.
  • Financial tensions were emphasised to make the father appear unstable.
  • Rumours about Hale's behaviour were allowed to circulate freely.

Multiple false stories. Multiple potential culprits. A safety net of misdirection.

Enough noise to hide the truth - unless someone external could cut through it.

The Letter You Received

At the end, you realise:

It was Jade who sent the anonymous packet of documents that triggered this entire investigation.

She pieced together what truly happened - eventually realising her sister had died in her place, because of a message intended for her.

Jade wanted justice. Or revenge. Or simply the truth to be seen by anyone outside those walls.

The Final Cost

But by the time help reached The Geminus Institute, Jade was beyond saving.

The induced symptoms, psychological stress, guilt, and manipulation had fractured her sense of self. The experiment had achieved its twisted goal:

the twin without a disorder now displayed its full set of symptoms - permanently.

Jasmine died for knowing too little. Jade broke for knowing too much.

And Dr Smith? Her reputation - her obsession - finally collapsed under the weight of her own deception.

Case Closed

The Geminus study is shut down. The cover-up is exposed. And the truth, as Joah hoped, can no longer be buried.

But the final image is not triumph - It is Joah, sitting alone in long-term psychiatric care, asking why she still hears her sister calling her name.

A final reminder of the devastating consequences of unethical research - and the terrible price of pursuing knowledge without humanity.
